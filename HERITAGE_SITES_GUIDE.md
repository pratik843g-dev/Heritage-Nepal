### 1. PASHUPATINATH TEMPLE
**Location:** Kathmandu, on banks of Bagmati River  
**Built:** 400 AD (current structure from 1692)  
**UNESCO Status:** Yes (1979)  
**Type:** Hindu Temple Complex  

**Historical Background:**
The temple's origins trace back to 400 AD, though the current structure was built in 1692 after termites destroyed the previous one. According to legend, Lord Shiva took the form of a deer (Pashupati means "Lord of Animals") and lived in the forests along the Bagmati River. When the gods found him, he jumped across the river, losing his horn which became the first Jyotirlinga at Pashupatinath.

**Religious Significance:**
- One of the most important Shiva temples in the world
- Considered one of the 275 Paadal Petra Sthalams
- Nepal's most sacred cremation site on the ghats
- Annual Maha Shivaratri attracts 100,000+ devotees from Nepal and India

**Architecture:**
- Classic Nepalese pagoda with two-tiered golden roof
- Four silver-plated doors with intricate deity carvings
- 518 mini-temples spread over 246 hectares
- The main Shiva Linga has four faces (Chaturmukhi)
- Survived 2015 earthquake with minimal damage

**Visiting Information:**
- **Timings:** 4:00 AM - 12:00 PM, 5:00 PM - 9:00 PM
- **Entry:** Free for Hindus, NPR 1,000 for non-Hindus
- **Best Time:** Early morning for Aarti, or during Maha Shivaratri (Feb/March)
- **Note:** Only Hindus allowed in main sanctum

**Key Facts:**
- Over 1,000 cremations performed annually
- Largest gold-plated roof in Nepal
- Ancient inscriptions date back to 5th century
- Managed by Pashupati Area Development Trust

### 2. BOUDHANATH STUPA
**Location:** Kathmandu, northeast of city center  
**Built:** 5th Century AD  
**UNESCO Status:** Yes (1979)  
**Type:** Buddhist Stupa  

**Historical Background:**
Built shortly after Buddha's death around the 5th century AD, believed to contain relics of Buddha Kashyapa. Following the 1959 Tibetan uprising, many Tibetan refugees settled around Boudhanath, creating a vibrant community of over 50 monasteries. The stupa was completely restored after the 2015 earthquake and reopened in November 2016.

**Religious Significance:**
- Center of Tibetan Buddhism in Kathmandu
- One of the largest spherical stupas in the world (120m diameter)
- Pilgrims circumambulate clockwise, spinning prayer wheels
- The all-seeing Buddha eyes represent omniscience

**Architecture:**
- Three-level mandala representing Mount Meru (Buddhist cosmos center)
- Base has 108 niches, each with Buddha statue
- 13-tiered spire symbolizes 13 steps to enlightenment
- Eyes painted with natural lapis lazuli pigment
- Over 400 prayer wheels surround the stupa

**Visiting Information:**
- **Timings:** Open 24 hours (best 5:00 AM - 10:00 PM)
- **Entry:** NPR 400 for foreigners
- **Best Time:** Dawn/dusk for prayers, or during Losar (Tibetan New Year)

**Key Facts:**
- Restored in just 18 months after 2015 earthquake
- 10,000+ butter lamps during festivals
- Generates NPR 100 million annually in revenue
- Home to over 50 Buddhist monasteries

### 3. SWAYAMBHUNATH (Monkey Temple)
**Location:** Kathmandu, hilltop west of city  
**Built:** 460 AD (site from 5th century BC)  
**UNESCO Status:** Yes (1979)  
**Type:** Buddhist Stupa Complex  

**Historical Background:**
According to Swayambhu Purana, Kathmandu Valley was once a lake. Bodhisattva Manjushri drained it with his sword, allowing worship of a lotus that grew where the stupa now stands. The stupa is believed to have been built by King Vrsadeva around 460 CE, though evidence suggests the site dates to the 5th century BC.

**Religious Significance:**
- Most sacred among Buddhist pilgrimage sites
- "Swayambhu" means self-arisen (naturally manifested)
- Revered by both Buddhists and Hindus
- Houses the oldest known Buddha image in Nepal

**Architecture:**
- White dome representing earth element
- Golden square tower (harmika) for air element  
- 13-tiered spire representing stages to enlightenment
- Umbrella symbolizing nirvana
- 365 steps leading to main stupa (representing one year)

**Visiting Information:**
- **Timings:** 5:00 AM - 8:00 PM
- **Entry:** NPR 200 for foreigners
- **Best Time:** Sunrise (6-7 AM) for valley views
- **Access:** 365 steps or vehicle road

**Key Facts:**
- Home to 200+ sacred monkeys
- 360-degree panoramic Kathmandu Valley views
- Library with 20,000 Buddhist manuscripts
- Restored after 2015 earthquake damage

### 4. KATHMANDU DURBAR SQUARE
**Location:** Central Kathmandu (Hanuman Dhoka)  
**Built:** 12th-18th Century  
**UNESCO Status:** Yes (1979)  
**Type:** Royal Palace Complex  

**Historical Background:**
The square holds the palaces of the Malla and Shah kings who ruled over Kathmandu. The oldest parts date from the 12th century during the Licchavi period. Most structures were built during the Malla dynasty (12th-18th centuries). The last king, Prithvi Narayan Shah, moved the capital here in 1768.

**Religious & Cultural Significance:**
- Home to the Kumari (Living Goddess)
- Served as seat of royal government until 1886
- Living museum of Newari culture (1000+ years)
- Hosts major festivals including Indra Jatra

**Architecture:**
- Over 50 temples and monuments
- Hanuman Dhoka Palace with 9 courtyards
- Taleju Temple (3-tiered pagoda, only open during Dashain)
- Kumari Ghar (house of Living Goddess)
- Kasthamandap (origin of Kathmandu's name - being rebuilt)

**Visiting Information:**
- **Timings:** 6:00 AM - 7:00 PM
- **Entry:** NPR 1,000 for foreigners (includes museum)
- **Best Time:** Early morning or during Indra Jatra (September)

**Key Facts:**
- Living Goddess Kumari appears at window daily
- Many structures damaged in 2015 earthquake
- Name "Kathmandu" derives from Kasthamandap temple
- Contains the oldest standing building in Nepal

### 5. PATAN DURBAR SQUARE (Lalitpur)
**Location:** Lalitpur (Patan), south of Kathmandu  
**Built:** 14th-17th Century  
**UNESCO Status:** Yes (1979)  
**Type:** Royal Palace Complex  

**Historical Background:**
Built by the Malla kings of Patan, the square reached its peak during King Siddhi Narasimha Malla's reign (1619-1661). Patan, meaning "City of Arts", was an independent kingdom until 1768 and has been a center for Buddhism and the arts for over 2000 years.

**Religious & Cultural Significance:**
- Finest collection of Newari temples and palaces in Nepal
- Known as "City of Arts" for exceptional craftsmanship
- Four Ashoka Stupas at cardinal points (marking Buddhist heritage)
- Harmonious blend of Buddhist and Hindu cultures

**Architecture:**
- Krishna Mandir: First stone temple in Nepal without nails/wood (21 pinnacles)
- Royal Palace with three main courtyards:
  - Mul Chowk
  - Sundari Chowk (famous Tusha Hiti stone bath)
  - Keshav Narayan Chowk
- Patan Museum (one of Asia's finest)
- 136 courtyards (bahals) and 55 major temples

**Visiting Information:**
- **Timings:** 7:00 AM - 7:00 PM
- **Entry:** NPR 1,000 for foreigners (includes Patan Museum)
- **Best Time:** October-March, during Krishna Janmashtami

**Key Facts:**
- Tusha Hiti is a masterpiece of stone carving
- Hosts famous Rato Machhendranath chariot festival
- Oldest surviving Buddhist monastery in valley
- Less earthquake damage than other squares

### 6. BHAKTAPUR DURBAR SQUARE
**Location:** Bhaktapur, 13 km east of Kathmandu  
**Built:** 12th-15th Century  
**UNESCO Status:** Yes (1979)  
**Type:** Royal Palace Complex  

**Historical Background:**
Founded in the 12th century by King Ananda Deva Malla. The city flourished as an independent kingdom until 1769. The Malla kings, particularly Bhupatindra Malla (1696-1722), created the magnificent monuments that stand today. Bhaktapur has maintained its ancient character better than any other city.

**Religious & Cultural Significance:**
- "City of Devotees" - best-preserved traditional Newari town
- Entire city is a living museum
- Famous for pottery, woodcarving, and juju dhau (yogurt)
- Strong preservation of medieval atmosphere

**Architecture:**
- **55-Window Palace (Pachpanna Jhyale Durbar):** Masterpiece of wood carving
- **Golden Gate (Sun Dhoka):** Over 1,000 kg of gold, repouss√© art masterpiece
- **Nyatapola Temple:** Tallest pagoda in Nepal (30m, five levels)
- **Vatsala Temple:** Famous stone bell
- **Peacock Window:** Symbol of Bhaktapur's artistry

**Visiting Information:**
- **Timings:** 7:00 AM - 7:00 PM
- **Entry:** NPR 1,500 for foreigners (valid for entire stay)
- **Best Time:** October-March, during Bisket Jatra (April - New Year)

**Key Facts:**
- Pottery Square shows unchanged traditional methods
- Famous for juju dhau in clay pots
- Most vibrant Nepali New Year celebration
- Less affected by modernization than Kathmandu

### 7. CHANGU NARAYAN TEMPLE
**Location:** Bhaktapur District, hilltop village  
**Built:** 323 AD (rebuilt 1702)  
**UNESCO Status:** Yes (1979)  
**Type:** Hindu Temple (Vishnu)  

**Historical Background:**
Nepal's oldest Hindu temple, built in 323 AD during the Licchavi period by King Haridatta Varma. Houses Nepal's oldest stone inscription (464 AD) from King Manadeva's reign. Despite being destroyed by fire in 1702, it was rebuilt maintaining the original style with Licchavi-era sculptures preserved.

**Religious & Cultural Significance:**
- Most ancient Hindu temple in Nepal (1,600+ years)
- Important Vaishnavite pilgrimage site
- Contains finest examples of Licchavi period craftsmanship
- Dedicated to Lord Vishnu as Garuda Narayan

**Architecture:**
- Classic two-tiered pagoda with intricate wood carvings
- Stone sculptures depicting 10 incarnations of Vishnu
- Ancient inscriptions providing valuable historical information
- Temple struts show various deities and mythological scenes
- Museum with ancient artifacts

**Visiting Information:**
- **Timings:** 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM
- **Entry:** NPR 300 for foreigners
- **Best Time:** October-March, during Haribodhini Ekadashi

**Key Facts:**
- Oldest inscription in Nepal (464 AD)
- Survived 2015 earthquake with minor damage
- Surround by traditional Newari houses
- Champak tree forest setting

### 8. LUMBINI - Birthplace of Buddha
**Location:** Rupandehi District (250 km SW of Kathmandu)  
**Built:** 623 BC (Buddha's birth)  
**UNESCO Status:** Yes (1997)  
**Type:** Buddhist Sacred Site  

**Historical Background:**
Prince Siddhartha Gautama was born in 623 BC in the Lumbini gardens to Queen Maya Devi of the Shakya kingdom. Emperor Ashoka visited in 249 BC and erected a pillar marking the exact birthplace. The site was lost for centuries and rediscovered in 1896 by Dr. Alois Anton F√ºhrer.

**Religious Significance:**
- One of four main Buddhist pilgrimage sites (birth, enlightenment, first sermon, death)
- Sacred to 500+ million Buddhists worldwide
- Symbol of peace, harmony, and compassion
- Exact birthplace of historical Buddha

**Site Components:**
- **Maya Devi Temple:** Marks exact birthplace with marker stone
- **Ashoka Pillar:** 2,200-year-old inscription in Brahmi script
- **Sacred Pond (Puskarni):** Where Maya Devi bathed before birth
- **Ancient Monastery Ruins:** From 3rd century BC
- **25+ International Monasteries:** Representing different Buddhist nations

**Visiting Information:**
- **Timings:** 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM (monasteries vary)
- **Entry:** Free to sacred garden
- **Best Time:** October-March, or Buddha Purnima (May)

**Key Facts:**
- Archaeological excavations confirm 3rd century BC structures
- World Peace Pagoda built by Japanese Buddhists
- Over 1 million pilgrims visit annually
- Lumbini Development Trust maintains 5 km¬≤ protected area

### 9. MUKTINATH TEMPLE
**Location:** Mustang District, altitude 3,710m  
**Built:** Ancient (exact date unknown)  
**Type:** Hindu & Buddhist Temple  

**Historical Background:**
A pilgrimage site for thousands of years, sacred to both Hindus and Buddhists. For Hindus, it's one of the 108 Divya Desams (sacred Vishnu temples) and an important Shakti Peetha. Buddhists call it Chumig Gyatsa meaning "Hundred Waters." The natural flame burning from natural gas is considered highly sacred.

**Religious Significance:**
- Rare temple where Hindus and Buddhists pray together
- Believed to grant mukti (salvation) to pilgrims
- Water from 108 taps believed to cleanse all sins
- Eternal flame with flowing water simultaneously is miraculous
- Part of the Annapurna trekking circuit

**Architecture:**
- Pagoda-style with gold-plated roof
- 108 bull-faced water spouts (Mukti Dhara) in courtyard
- Two sacred ponds: Saraswati Kund and Laxmi Kund
- Buddhist gompa nearby
- Images of both Vishnu and Buddhist deities

**Visiting Information:**
- **Timings:** 5:00 AM - 7:00 PM (earlier closing in winter)
- **Entry:** Free
- **Best Time:** March-June, September-November
- **Access:** By trek or road from Jomsom

**Key Facts:**
- Eternal flame represents combination of five elements
- Shaligram stones from nearby Kali Gandaki River
- Extreme weather conditions
- Must-visit on Annapurna Circuit
- At 3,710m altitude, can be challenging

### 10. JANAKI MANDIR TEMPLE
**Location:** Janakpur, Dhanusha District  
**Built:** 1910  
**Type:** Hindu Temple (dedicated to Sita)  

**Historical Background:**
Built in 1910 by Queen Vrisha Bhanu of Tikamgarh, India. Janakpur is the ancient capital of the Kingdom of Mithila, ruled by King Janak (Sita's father). Archaeological excavations confirm ancient settlements dating to the 2nd century BC. This marks the birthplace of Goddess Sita.

**Religious Significance:**
- Marks birthplace of Goddess Sita (wife of Lord Rama)
- Second most important Hindu pilgrimage site in Nepal after Pashupatinath
- Major site for Ramayana devotees
- Vivaha Mandap marks the spot of Sita-Ram marriage

**Architecture:**
- Three-storied structure with 60 rooms
- Mughal and Rajput architectural styles
- Made entirely of stone and marble imported from Rajasthan
- Intricate carvings, pillars, and domes
- Largest temple in Nepal

**Visiting Information:**
- **Timings:** 5:30 AM - 9:00 PM
- **Entry:** Free
- **Best Time:** During Vivaha Panchami (November/December)

**Key Facts:**
- Built entirely of stone and marble
- Vivaha Panchami attracts 100,000+ devotees
- Only temple in Nepal with Mughal architecture
- Surrounded by 54 ponds including Ganga Sagar

### 11. MANAKAMANA TEMPLE
**Location:** Gorkha District, altitude 1,302m  
**Built:** 17th Century  
**Type:** Hindu Temple (Goddess Bhagwati)  

**Historical Background:**
Built in the 17th century during the reign of Gorkha King Ram Shah. According to legend, the goddess manifested from the stone of a revered saint named Lakhan Thapa. The temple's name derives from "mana" (heart) and "kamana" (wish), meaning "wishes of the heart."

**Religious Significance:**
- Believed to grant wishes of devotees who worship with sincere heart
- Important pilgrimage site for newlyweds seeking blessings
- Popular for those starting new ventures
- Goddess Bhagwati is incarnation of Parvati

**Architecture:**
- Traditional pagoda-style with two-tiered roof
- Stone structure with intricate wood carvings
- Goddess Bhagwati image inside temple
- Surrounded by smaller deity shrines

**Visiting Information:**
- **Timings:** 6:00 AM - 7:00 PM
- **Entry:** Free (Cable car: NPR 1,000 roundtrip for foreigners)
- **Access:** 10-minute cable car or 3-hour uphill walk
- **Best Time:** September-November, during Dashain

**Key Facts:**
- First cable car in Nepal (opened 1998)
- 2.8 km cable car ride
- Animal sacrifice practiced (goats, roosters)
- Spectacular Himalayan views from temple
- Newlyweds visit for marriage blessings

### 12. GOSAIKUNDA LAKE
**Location:** Rasuwa District, altitude 4,380m  
**Built:** Natural formation  
**Type:** Sacred Alpine Lake  

**Historical Background:**
According to Hindu mythology, Lord Shiva created this lake by thrusting his trident into the mountain to obtain water to cool his burning throat after swallowing poison (halahala) during the churning of the ocean. The lake is mentioned in the Bhagavata Purana and Ramayana.

**Religious Significance:**
- One of Nepal's most sacred lakes
- Water believed to be direct link to Lord Shiva
- Thousands of pilgrims visit during Janai Purnima (August)
- Sacred to Hindus, Buddhists, and Shamanists

**Site Details:**
- Natural glacial lake in Langtang National Park
- Main Gosaikunda surrounded by 107 other lakes
- Connected lakes: Saraswati Kunda, Bhairav Kunda, Surya Kunda
- Stone temple dedicated to Shiva on northern shore
- Source of Trishuli River

**Visiting Information:**
- **Access:** 2-3 day trek from Dhunche
- **Best Time:** Janai Purnima (August full moon)
- **Season:** March-May, September-November
- **Entry:** NPR 3,000 TIMS + Langtang National Park fee

**Key Facts:**
- Created by Shiva's trident according to mythology
- Frozen in winter months
- Janai Purnima attracts 10,000+ pilgrims
- Lake appears turquoise in summer
- At 4,380m - altitude sickness precautions needed

### 13. DAKSHINKALI TEMPLE
**Location:** Pharping, 22 km south of Kathmandu  
**Built:** Ancient (current from 14th century)  
**Type:** Hindu Temple (Goddess Kali)  

**Historical Background:**
Temple dates back centuries, with current structure from the 14th century. According to legend, King Pratap Malla had a vision of the goddess directing him to her image in the forest. The temple marks a spot where Goddess Sati's body parts fell during Lord Shiva's tandava dance, making it a Shakti Peetha.

**Religious Significance:**
- One of the main Shakti Peethas in Nepal
- Goddess Kali worshipped as protector and destroyer of evil
- Animal sacrifice performed on Tuesdays and Saturdays
- Important pilgrimage site for Kali devotees

**Architecture:**
- Open-air shrine without roof
- Black stone image of Goddess Kali with ten arms
- Standing on prostrate male figure (representing ego)
- Set in gorge at confluence of two streams
- Stone steps lead down to temple courtyard

**Visiting Information:**
- **Timings:** 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM (busiest Tuesday/Saturday)
- **Entry:** Free
- **Best Time:** Tuesday or Saturday mornings, or during Dashain
- **Note:** Animal sacrifices performed - sensitive subject

**Key Facts:**
- Animal sacrifices (roosters, goats) twice weekly
- Black stone Kali with ten arms
- Confluence of two streams
- Thousands visit during Dashain
- Blood from sacrifices flows into streams

### 14. BUDHANILKANTHA (Sleeping Vishnu)
**Location:** 9 km north of Kathmandu  
**Built:** 7th-8th Century (Licchavi period)  
**Type:** Hindu Temple (Vishnu)  

**Historical Background:**
Created during the Licchavi period (7th-8th century AD), though the exact date is unknown. According to legend, the statue was discovered by a farmer plowing his field. The mystery of how the massive single-piece statue (5 meters long) was transported and carved remains unsolved. The name "Budhanilkantha" means "Old Blue Throat" referring to Vishnu (not Buddha as commonly misunderstood).

**Religious Significance:**
- Largest stone carving of Vishnu in Nepal
- Depicts Vishnu in Ananta Shayana (cosmic sleep) position
- Lying on bed of eleven-headed serpent Shesha
- Important pilgrimage site for Vaishnavites
- Taboo: Kings of Nepal never visit (legend says they'll die)

**Architecture:**
- 5-meter long reclining Vishnu statue in pond
- Single piece of black basalt stone
- Four arms holding Vishnu's attributes:
  - Chakra (discus)
  - Conch shell
  - Mace
  - Lotus flower
- Lying on coiled serpent Ananta

**Visiting Information:**
- **Timings:** 4:00 AM - 7:00 PM
- **Entry:** Free
- **Best Time:** Early morning, during Haribodhini Ekadashi (Oct/Nov)

**Key Facts:**
- Largest stone carving of deity in Nepal
- Single piece of black basalt
- Mystery of transportation and carving
- Annual bath ceremony (ritual cleaning)
- Kings never visit due to ancient legend

### Purpose
These QR codes will serve as permanent cultural preservation tools, allowing visitors to learn about Nepal's heritage without guides, ensuring history is passed to future generations.

### QR Code Content
Each QR code should contain: `heritage-nepal:SITE-ID`

Example: `heritage-nepal:pashupatinath`

### Physical Installation Guidelines

**Materials:**
- Print on weather-resistant material or laminate
- Use UV-resistant ink
- Mount on durable backing (aluminum, acrylic, or treated wood)
- Include protective covering for outdoor installations

**Placement:**
- At main entrance/ticket counter
- Near information boards
- At 5-6 feet height (eye level)
- Well-lit area
- Protected from direct rain if possible
- Clean, unobstructed area

**Signage Should Include:**
- QR code (250x250px minimum)
- Site name in Nepali and English
- Brief instruction: "Scan to learn history"
- Nepal Heritage Explorer logo

### Maintenance
- Check quarterly for damage
- Clean regularly
- Replace if faded or damaged
- Update if information changes
